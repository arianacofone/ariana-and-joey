See & Do

Below are a couple of places to check out near our venue, but NYC is your oyster! Just get lost and have fun.

Chelsea Market | Website

An indoor market with a tons of small vendors displaying their wears. A great place to wander and for a quick meal!

The Highline | Website

An old elevated railroad line that was transformed into 1.45 miles of outdoor space with installation pieces, greenery, and of course the NYC skyline.

"The Whitney" Museum of Art | Website

2,000 square feet of art focusing on pieces from the 20th-21st century. There's also outdoor observation decks on the 5th, 6th, and 7th floor to a view of the city.

Union Square | Website

A park dropped down in the middle of it all! On Fridays and Saturdays, there's a beautiful market with fresh foods and beautiful wears.

Ground Zero Museum | Website

A piece of history captured in an incredibly powerful museum.

Soul Cycle Chelsea | Website

If you like spinning, you gotta take a class at Soul Cycle. NYC spinners swear by it for a good workout and great time.

Eat

The Park | Website

A great place for brunch or dinner, your dining atmosphere is a glassed-in garden & a rooftop patio overlooking the highline.

Milk Bar Chelsea | Website

A bakery known for its quirky and delicious desserts (our favorite...cereal milk ice cream). A perfect summer treat.

Fat Cat | Website

A haunt of NYU students, this underground bar serves us some great jazz as you indulge in a bunch of different games (from board games to pool).

Dos Caminos | Website

A lively cantina serving modern Mexican cuisine with solid guacamole and knock you on your booty tequila cocktails.

The Grey Dog | Website

A casual coffeehouse serving all meals from breakfast to dinner. Oh and the pricing is a nice break from the NYC craziness.

Art Bar | Website

A dive bar with good drinks and solid bar food. A fun local spot for those looking to not break the bank on drinks.
